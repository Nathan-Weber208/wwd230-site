Principle 1:Alignment

Alignment is a principle that is never appreciated but is infuriating when seen done wrong. Alignment is when your elements on your webpage line up, have good spacing, and don't overlap.

Look at In-and-Out's website to the right. You see it plainly in the images below but even in the floating text boxes they are justified and in conformity. When your eye looks over it, you are not annoyed by a 10 pixel difference in hight or width.

In-and-Out's Homepage

Principle 2:Whitespace

The principle of Whitespace is exactly what it sounds like. Whitespace (or any background color) is used to make your information look more important.

Look at McDonald's homepage to the left. Here they use whitespace to show this new coupon for fries. they could have used that room for advertizing or for something else. it really could have been anything they are a multi-billion dollar company. Instead its blank so our attention is not pulled from what they want us to see.

McDonald's Homepage

Principle 3:Hick's Law

Hick's Law is a simple design principle that is easy to follow. Don't give the user too many options. Your webpage should not look like a cockpit with buttons and toggles everywhere. Rather give the user a very straight-forward path as not to be confused.

Doorzza's example is a comical extreme. When you load up the webpage you are shown your choice: "Order Now" There is no other reason for you to look up this site. Sure if you scroll around you'll find some more options but the intended path is clear.
